# PC Build (Feb 26)

## Objective

My goal is to build a PC that is great to game on, as well as to be the center of my future projects, which will include:

1. Homelabbing  
2. Streaming  
3. Studying for certifications  

---

## Design Philosophy

1. The computer must be aircooled to keep maintenance to a minimum.  
2. I must have sufficient storage for OS to grow, game library to be comfortable on, and plenty of cold storage for assets to accumulate.  
3. It must have precise aircooling to run as cool as possible to keep performance at its peak.  
4. It must have slight positive pressure to keep dust from accumulating.

## Peripherals

Using the computer must be a joy, as I will be putting lots of hours on this machine.

Monitor - Ultrawide monitor makes gaming feel new, as well as being 3 monitors without the center bezel.  
![Monitor](./Images%20Folder/Monitor.png)

Keyboard - Nuphy Node 100 in Beige w/ brown switches  
The color reminds me of computers from when I was a child in the nineties, and the thocky keys sound like popcorn while I type which is pleasant for me.  
Since I am not in the office, I can be happy with a noisier keyboard.  
![Keyboard](./Images%20Folder/Keyboard.png)

Mouse - Pulsar Xlite Crazylight  
I have never used an ultra light mouse it too is beige in color, this is a new one for me, I forget that my hand is sitting on something because of how little force it takes to move.  
I do not believe this makes me a better gamer, but it is pleasant to use.  
![Mouse](./Images%20Folder/Mouse.png)

DAC - Schiit Audio Gunnr  
I am an audiophile and I have never had a DAC on my computer just to handle sound. On my studio setup I have a DAC that handles both inputs and outputs of audio for audio / music production.  
This pairs nicely with my headphones and makes controlling the loudness a more tactile experience and drives them to way higher volumes.  
![DAC](./Images%20Folder/DAC.png)

HeadPhones - Audeze LCD-GX  
These headphones are really comfortable and drive crystal clear sound, and their open back nature allows me to hear things in the room at low volumes.  
![Headphones](./Images%20Folder/heaphones.png)

## Lessons Learned

- This was not as challenging as I expected it to be. I have a few years of IT experience and have gotten to work on laptops, but never a full desktop build.  
  I expected this to be way harder, the week of I watched videos to prep myself, I only had to remove parts twice to get everything to fit.  
  I was pleasantly surprised whenever the PC turned on and was ready for configurations.

- My blocker was installing the OS because my motherboard could not see my storage until I got the drivers for them.  
  ChatGPT helped me out of this bind, I used it to quickly generate possible issues that it could be, and on the second attempt it worked, first problem was saying because I had multiple drives installed it may be confusing the motherboard, so I left the OS drive and proceeded to install the drivers.

- Once the OS was installed I treated it as though I was at work and did all of the Windows updates, set startup apps, ran my Ninite installer loaded with the programs I wanted, ran `winget upgrade --all`, then once that was done I went and turned EXPO on to get all of the RAM speeds.

- I am a big fan of ultrawide monitors, gaming is more immersive in my opinion and it is like having two monitors really close so I have to do minimal neck turning.
  
- The mouse is okay, I am considering going back to the Razer Basilisk, it has not been too much of a draw but it is a thought I have had.
  
- Keyboard trays are underrated.
